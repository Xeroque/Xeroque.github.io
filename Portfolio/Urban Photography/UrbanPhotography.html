The urban jungle is a dauting place, navigating my way through this metropolitan area with almost 6 million inhabitants I was tasked to take pictures that embody some of Gestalt’s principles for visual creation. These principles are the rules that dictate design in the modern world, from photography to architecture passing trough web design and advertisement. Let me show you some of the pictures I took and how they capture select Gestalt principles.

The photo above is a classic example of the principle of closure, the underexposed post in the centre of the image breaks the light sun setting sky, but our object permanence connects the two visible parts of the sky, leaving us with “free real state” to place branding or any text on.Hereyou can see this same principle being used on a Adidas add by Mel Blanchard. I this image is also the principle of closure (as the sky is being framed by the buildings) but we’ll talk about them later.
The next image follows the principle of figure-ground perception, this is our brains separating discernible shapes into regions of interest, on this particular case the image is split in half diagonally between the white sky and the buildings, this can also be observed on an Adidas ad,here.
