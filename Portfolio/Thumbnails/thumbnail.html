The urban jungle is a dauting place, navigating my way through this metropolitan area with almost 6 million inhabitants I was tasked to take pictures that embody some of Gestalt’s principles for visual creation. These principles are the rules that dictate design in the modern world, from photography to architecture passing trough web design and advertisement. Let me show you some of the pictures I took and how they capture select Gestalt principles.

The photo above is a classic example of the principle of closure, the underexposed post in the centre of the image breaks the light sun setting sky, but our object permanence connects the two visible parts of the sky, leaving us with “free real state” to place branding or any text on. Here you can see this same principle being used on a Adidas add by Mel Blanchard.
The next image follows the principle of figure-ground perception, this is our brains separating discernible shapes into regions of interest, on this particular case the image is split in half diagonally between the white sky and the buildings, this can also be observed on an Adidas ad, here.

The two images of the buildings receiving renovations are a example of the principle of similarity, the repetition of the floors and windows makes you see them as a group, so much so that if not by the clipling of the sky on top of the right picture you may had not have realized they were two photos. This time Nike has made an ad with a similar concept, you can watch it here.
The next image is another example of the concept of closure,my favorite Gestalt principle, framing the building by the space between others you get a new perception on scale, position and angle.
