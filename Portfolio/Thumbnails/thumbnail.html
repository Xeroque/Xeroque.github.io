On the eCommerce market, as I've talked about on my product photography page,image is everything.
But on a more practical note, not all images can, nor should, be artsy intricate and expensive to make pieces. Most of the time you’re going to need pieces in bulk, made fast, that can convey what your product is, are good for SEO and fast to load while also being attention grabbing, in short, thumbnails. In this page I’m going to show you some pieces that I made following these principles, common obstacles and how I managed to work my way around them.

Here we can see what can be done when you’ve got great source images, the photos above and bellow were made fast to match the aesthetic of the existing website.

When conditions are not ideal, the original product photos are low quality, have bad lighting or the product is crocked you have some leeway to move and experiment with repetition, text and calls to action like those seen here.

The two images of the buildings receiving renovations are a example of the principle of similarity, the repetition of the floors and windows makes you see them as a group, so much so that if not by the clipling of the sky on top of the right picture you may had not have realized they were two photos. This time Nike has made an ad with a similar concept, you can watch it here.
The next image is another example of the concept of closure,my favorite Gestalt principle, framing the building by the space between others you get a new perception on scale, position and angle.
